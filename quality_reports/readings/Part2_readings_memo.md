### Replace

**REPLACE 1: Devlin & Hansen (2000) NZ Treasury Working Paper 00/4**

This paper is cited but not readily accessible (Treasury website does not surface it in search; likely in physical archives). Replace with a more accessible and recent NZ economic evaluation piece:

> **Replacement — NZ evaluation framework:**
> Grocott, R. & Metcalfe, S. (2013). "PHARMAC's cost-utility analysis: guidelines updated." *New Zealand Medical Journal*, 126(1378), 1–12. Available at: https://nzmj.org.nz/media/pages/journal/vol-126-no-1378/assessing-the-value-for-money-of-pharmaceuticals-in-new-zealand/

However, the NZMJ piece is about methods updates; a better accessible replacement for the Devlin & Hansen framing paper (how should NZ allocate Vote: Health?) is:

> Devlin, N. (2014). "Health economics and health policy: experiences from New Zealand." *Applied Health Economics and Health Policy*, 12(6), 585–598. DOI: [10.1007/s40258-014-0149-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40258-014-0149-y)

- **Why better:** More recent (2014), peer-reviewed, accessible via Springer. Written by Nancy Devlin (who also co-authored the original 2000 Treasury paper). Covers NZ's experience with PHARMAC, cost-per-QALY thresholds, and the policy rationale — directly addressing what the 2000 working paper was trying to establish but in a much more accessible, peer-reviewed form.
- **Availability:** Springer paywall — Otago institutional access likely.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED — AHEHP 12(6):585-598, DOI confirmed on SpringerLink.

**REPLACE 2: Salvatore Ch. 14 excerpts (discounting)**

Salvatore is a microeconomics principles textbook — the discounting excerpt is pedagogically fine but generic. Replace with the PHARMAC free online guidance on discounting which is NZ-specific and freely available, OR keep a 2-page explanation from Drummond et al. (which is already on the list). Better solution: drop Salvatore entirely and let W08D3 discounting content be self-contained in the lecture with Drummond et al. Ch. 2 providing the methodological backdrop.

**REPLACE 3: *The Economist* "Apocalypse maybe" (March 1996)**

This piece is about uncertainty in economic forecasts (climate change context). While the concept of uncertainty applies to health decisions, this is an awkward fit. Replace with:

> *The Economist* (2012). "Priceless" — or the relevant section from the Economist's Health Care special report. **UNVERIFIED — Austin to identify a preferred Economist piece on discounting or uncertainty in health contexts.** Alternatively, this slot can be dropped entirely (the discounting concepts are sufficiently covered in the lecture + Drummond et al.).

**Recommended for dropping (reduce load):** "Apocalypse maybe" (1996) and the Salvatore excerpt. Replace with the shorter Devlin (2014) AHEHP piece.

**ADD 2 — Fair innings / equity consideration (optional, W09D2)**

The fair innings argument and equity in health evaluation are core to W09D2. The current reading set does not have a specific reading for this. Propose:

> Williams, A. (1997). "Intergenerational equity: an exploration of the 'fair innings' argument." *Health Economics*, 6(2), 117–132. DOI: [10.1002/(SICI)1099-1050(199703)6:2<117::AID-HEC256>3.0.CO;2-B](https://doi.org/10.1002/(SICI)1099-1050(199703)6:2<117::AID-HEC256>3.0.CO;2-B)

- **Why:** Alan Williams coined the "fair innings" concept — this is the primary source and it's only 16 pp, accessible, and directly teaches the equity dimension of CUA. Standard assignment in health economics courses.
- **Availability:** Wiley paywall — Otago institutional access. ResearchGate has accessible version.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED — Health Economics 6(2):117-132, 1997; DOI confirmed on Wiley Online Library.
- **Recommendation:** Optional (students doing the equity/fair innings assignment question should read this).
