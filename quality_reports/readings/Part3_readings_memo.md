> NZ Productivity Commission (2017). *New Models of Tertiary Education: Final Report*. Wellington: New Zealand Productivity Commission. Available at: https://www.treasury.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2024-05/pc-inq-nmte-final-report-tertiary-education-v2.pdf

Free, authoritative, government-commissioned.
- **Recommendation:** Required for W10D2 (NZ education system + student loans) — assign the Executive Summary (~20 pp) or specific chapters on student loans (Ch. 6 in the draft/final). Short enough for undergraduate reading.

### Add

**ADD 1 — NZ student loan economics: interest-free design (required, W10D2)**

Austin's TOP PRIORITY: readings on the reasoning behind NZ education system structures, especially the student loan scheme. The best accessible, verified reading:

> New Zealand Initiative (2014). *Decade of Debt: The Cost of Interest-Free Student Loans*. Wellington: NZ Initiative. Available at: https://www.nzinitiative.org.nz/reports-and-media/reports/decade-of-debt-the-cost-of-interest-free-student-loans/

- **Why:** Economic analysis of the NZ interest-free student loan policy from an economic perspective — costs, incentive effects, distributional consequences. Accessible, short (~40 pp), free, NZ-specific. Contrarian perspective (recommends reintroducing interest) that pairs well with the Productivity Commission report to give students both sides.
- **Recommendation:** Optional for W10D2 — alternative perspective that generates debate.
- **Availability:** Open access, free download.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED — NZ Initiative 2014 report confirmed at URL.

**ADD 2 — Income-contingent loans theory (optional, W10D2)**

For students who want to understand the economic rationale for income-contingent repayment more deeply:

> Barr, N. (2004). "Higher education funding." *Oxford Review of Economic Policy*, 20(2), 264–283. DOI: [10.1093/oxrep/grh015](https://doi.org/10.1093/oxrep/grh015)

- **Why:** Nicholas Barr (LSE) is the leading theorist of income-contingent student loans. This article explains *why* income-contingent repayment solves the human capital market failure — directly relevant to W10D2 objective of explaining the economics of income-contingent student loans. Pre-print available: https://eprints.lse.ac.uk/30873/
- **Recommendation:** Optional for W10D2 (especially useful for students writing on student loan policy).
- **Availability:** Oxford Review of Economic Policy — paywall; Otago institutional access. LSE pre-print is open access.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED — OXREP 20(2):264-283, DOI 10.1093/oxrep/grh015 confirmed.

> Spence, M. (1973). "Job market signaling." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 87(3), 355–374. DOI: [10.2307/1882010](https://doi.org/10.2307/1882010)

- **Why:** The foundational Nobel Prize paper that is explicitly taught in W11D1. Students in a 300-level course should read the original (at least the first 10 pp establishing the model). Nobel 2001 — a landmark paper that is now 50+ years old and directly teachable.
- **Availability:** JSTOR/QJE — paywall; Otago institutional access. Free PDF at UCLA: https://competitionandappropriation.econ.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/95/2017/08/Spence1973.pdf and SFU: https://www.sfu.ca/~allen/Spence.pdf
- **Recommendation:** Required for W11D1 (pp. 355–365 at minimum — the model section).
- **Verification:** VERIFIED — QJE 87(3):355-374, DOI confirmed; multiple free PDFs found.

**ADD 2 — Sheepskin effects (optional, W11D2)**

> Hungerford, T. & Solon, G. (1987). "Sheepskin effects in the returns to education." *Review of Economics and Statistics*, 69(1), 175–177. DOI: [10.2307/1937919](https://doi.org/10.2307/1937919)

- **Why:** Classic 3-page paper providing the original sheepskin effect evidence — direct empirical test of signalling vs. human capital. Short enough for required reading; foundational for the W11D2 lecture on evidence.
- **Availability:** JSTOR/REST — paywall; Otago institutional access.
- **Recommendation:** Optional (but highly recommended for students interested in the signalling evidence).
- **Verification:** VERIFIED — REST 69(1):175-177, 1987; confirmed on Semantic Scholar and ResearchGate.
